1 — Needs Improvement: Very few or no students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Very few or no students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations. Students do not execute transitions, routines and procedures in an orderly manner. Students are left without work for a significant portion of the class period.

2 — Emerging: Some students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Some students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations. Students execute transitions only some of the time and/or require substantial direction from the teacher. Students are idle for one or two minutes at a time.

3 — Developing: Most students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Most students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations and directions. Students execute transitions in an orderly and efficient manner most of the time, though they may require some direction. Students are idle for short periods (<1 min).

4 — Proficient: All or almost all students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. All or almost all students follow behavioral expectations. Students execute transitions in an orderly and efficient manner with minimal direction. Class has a quick pace and students are engaged from start to finish. Students who finish early engage in meaningful learning.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and: Students assume responsibility for routines and procedures in an orderly, efficient and self-directed manner, requiring no direction from the teacher. Students demonstrate a sense of ownership of behavioral expectations by holding each other accountable.

5 — Exemplary: Level 4 met + students exhibit strong problem solving skills that enhance interactions. Students participate in Kagan at an exemplary level.

2 — Emerging: Some students provide evidence. Students complete some cognitive work but the teacher or very few students complete most. Students do not respond to peers or provide feedback. Some try hard. Discussion/writing 35-40%.

3 — Developing: Most students provide evidence. Students complete an appropriately challenging amount of cognitive work. Students respond to peers and provide feedback. Most try hard even if challenging. Discussion/writing ≥50%.

4 — Proficient: All or almost all provide evidence. Students respond to and build on peers' thinking. Routinely provide constructive feedback and respond productively to incorrect answers. All consistently try hard. Discussion/writing >50%.

5 — Exemplary: Level 4 met + students make inferences, draw conclusions, solve problems in multiple ways. Students independently show enthusiasm for advanced or more challenging content.
