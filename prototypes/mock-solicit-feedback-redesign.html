Flow summary
  • Email mode: requestor submits → 1 email to teacher with question(s) + secure link → teacher answers on response page → submits → (a) gratitude email to teacher, (b) summary email to requestor with their responses, (c) record stored on teacher's profile.
  • In-person mode: requestor types responses inline as the conversation happens → submits → 1 gratitude email to teacher → record stored on teacher's profile (no email back to requestor; they have it on screen).
  • Multi-select cap: 3 questions max. Once 3 are picked, remaining options grey out.
  • Likert scales (sustainability + flight risk) are part of the request itself, not a private read. In email mode, Drew rates them on the response page. In in-person mode, the requestor records what Drew said. Always asked — they're standardized pulse items.
  • Public response link: uses a token like the PIP/Write-Up acknowledgment URL. No login needed for Drew. Only the link-holder can post the response.
  • Privacy line on response page: "Only Scott and admins can see what you wrote." — manages expectations.
Question management — easy editing
  • Source of truth: new solicit_questions.yaml at project root, same pattern as permissions.yaml.
  • Structure: 
    questions:
  - "How is your workload right now?"
  - "How are you feeling about your goals?"
  - "What support do you need from me?"
  - "How is the team culture feeling?"
  - "What's one thing I can do better?"
allow_custom: true   # show "Custom question…" option
  • Day-1: Scott edits the YAML and redeploys. 5 minutes per change. (Same way permissions.yaml works.)
  • Later (post-launch): add an admin page at /app/admin/questions that reads/writes the same YAML. Add/edit/remove/reorder via UI. ~45 min when needed.
  • Form loads questions from /api/solicit-questions on mount — admins always see latest list without rebuilding the frontend bundle.
