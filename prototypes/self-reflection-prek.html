▶ View descriptors
Positive Climate: Reflects the emotional connection between the teacher and students and among students and the warmth, respect, and enjoyment communicated by verbal and nonverbal interactions

Relationships (Physical proximity, Shared activities, Peer assistance, Matched affect, Social conversation)
Low: There are few, if any, indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another
Mid: There are some indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another
High: There are many indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another

Positive Affect (Smiling, Laughter, Enthusiasm)
Low: There are no or few displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students
Mid: There are sometimes displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students
High: There are frequent displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students

Positive Communication (Verbal affection, Physical affection, Positive expectations)
Low: There are rarely positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students
Mid: There are sometimes positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students
High: There are frequently positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students

Respect (Eye contact, Warm, calm voice, Respectful language, Cooperation and/or sharing)
Low: The teacher and students rarely, if ever, demonstrate respect for one another
Mid: The teacher and students sometimes demonstrate respect for one another
High: The teacher and students consistently demonstrate respect for one another

Negative Climate: Reflects the overall level of expressed negativity in the classroom; the frequency, quality, and intensity of teacher and peer negativity are key to this scale

Negative Climate is scaled in the opposite direction. Higher negativity indicates lower quality.

Negative Affect (Irritability, Anger, Harsh voice, Peer aggression, Disconnected or escalating negativity)
Low: The teacher and students do not display strong negative affect and only rarely, if ever, display mild negativity
Mid: The classroom is characterized by mild displays of irritability, anger, or other negative affect by the teacher and/or students
High: The classroom is characterized by consistent irritability, anger, or other negative affect by the teacher and/or the students

Punitive Control (Yelling, Threats, Physical control, Harsh punishment)
Low: The teacher does not yell or make threats to establish control
Mid: The teacher occasionally uses expressed negativity such as threats or yelling to establish control
High: The teacher repeatedly yells at students or makes threats to establish control

Sarcasm/Disrespect (Sarcastic voice/statement, Teasing, Humiliation)
Low: The teacher and students are not sarcastic or disrespectful
Mid: The teacher and/or students are occasionally sarcastic or disrespectful
High: The teacher and/or students are repeatedly sarcastic or disrespectful

Severe Negativity (Victimization, Bullying, Physical punishment)
Low: There are no instances of severe negativity between the teacher and students
Mid: There are no instances of severe negativity between the teacher and students
High: There are instances of severe negativity between the teacher and students or among the students

▶ View descriptors
Teacher Sensitivity: Encompasses the teacher's awareness of and responsivity to students' academic and emotional needs; high levels of sensitivity facilitate students' ability to actively explore and learn because the teacher consistently provides comfort, reassurance, and encouragement

Awareness (Anticipates problems and plans appropriately, Notices lack of understanding and/or difficulties)
Low: The teacher consistently fails to be aware of students who need extra support, assistance, or attention
Mid: The teacher is sometimes aware of students who need extra support, assistance, or attention
High: The teacher is consistently aware of students who need extra support, assistance, or attention

Responsiveness (Acknowledges emotions, Provides comfort and assistance, Provides individualized support)
Low: The teacher is unresponsive to or dismissive of students and provides the same level of assistance to all students, regardless of their individual needs
Mid: The teacher is responsive to students sometimes but at other times is more dismissive or unresponsive, matching support to the needs of some students but not others
High: The teacher is consistently responsive to students and matches support to their needs and abilities

Addresses Problems (Helps in an effective and timely manner, Helps resolve problems)
Low: The teacher is ineffective at addressing students' problems and concerns
Mid: The teacher is sometimes effective at addressing students' problems and concerns
High: The teacher is consistently effective at addressing students' problems and concerns

Student Comfort (Seeks support and guidance, Freely participates, Takes risks)
Low: The students rarely seek support, share their ideas with, or respond to questions from the teacher
Mid: The students sometimes seek support from, share their ideas with, or respond to questions from the teacher
High: The students appear comfortable seeking support from, sharing their ideas with, and responding freely to the teacher

Regard for Student Perspectives: Captures the degree to which the teacher's interactions with students and classroom activities place an emphasis on students' interests, motivations, and points of view and encourage student responsibility and autonomy

Flexibility and Student Focus (Shows flexibility, Incorporates students' ideas, Follows students' lead)
Low: The teacher is rigid, inflexible, and controlling; most classroom activities are teacher-driven
Mid: The teacher may follow the students' lead during some periods and be more controlling during others
High: The teacher is flexible, goes along with students' ideas, and organizes instruction around students' interests

Support for Autonomy and Leadership (Allows choice, Allows students to lead lessons, Gives students responsibility)
Low: The teacher does not support student autonomy and leadership
Mid: The teacher sometimes provides support for student autonomy and leadership but at other times fails to do so
High: The teacher provides consistent support for student autonomy and leadership

Student Expression (Encourages student talk, Elicits ideas and/or perspectives)
Low: There are few opportunities for student talk and expression
Mid: There are periods with a lot of student talk and expression but other times when teacher talk predominates
High: There are many opportunities for student talk and expression

Restriction of Movement (Allows movement, Is not rigid)
Low: The teacher is highly controlling of students' movement and placement during activities
Mid: The teacher is somewhat controlling of students' movement and placement during activities
High: The students have freedom of movement and placement during activities

Behavior Management

▶ View descriptors
Behavior Management: Encompasses the teacher's ability to provide clear behavioral expectations and use effective methods to prevent and redirect misbehavior

Clear Behavior Expectations (Clear expectations, Consistency, Clarity of rules)
Low: Rules and expectations are absent, unclear, or inconsistently enforced
Mid: Rules and expectations may be stated clearly but are inconsistently enforced
High: Rules and expectations for behavior are clear and consistently enforced

Proactive (Anticipates problem behavior or escalation, Low reactivity, Monitors)
Low: The teacher is reactive, and monitoring is absent or ineffective
Mid: The teacher uses a mix of proactive and reactive responses; sometimes monitors and reacts to early indicators but other times misses or ignores them
High: The teacher is consistently proactive and monitors the classroom effectively to prevent problems from developing

Redirection of Misbehavior (Effective reduction of misbehavior, Attention to the positive, Uses subtle cues to redirect, Efficient redirection)
Low: Attempts to redirect misbehavior are ineffective; the teacher rarely focuses on positives or uses subtle cues. Misbehavior continues and/or escalates
Mid: Some attempts to redirect are effective, particularly when focusing on positive behavior and using subtle cues. Few times when misbehavior continues or escalates
High: The teacher effectively redirects misbehavior by focusing on positives and using subtle cues. Behavior management does not take time away from learning

Student Behavior (Frequent compliance, Little aggression and defiance)
Low: There are frequent instances of misbehavior in the classroom
Mid: There are periodic episodes of misbehavior in the classroom
High: There are few, if any, instances of student misbehavior in the classroom

▶ View descriptors
Productivity: Considers how well the teacher manages instructional time and routines and provides activities for students so that they have the opportunity to be involved in learning activities

Maximizing Learning Time (Provision of activities, Choice when finished, Few disruptions, Effective completion of managerial tasks, Pacing)
Low: Few, if any, activities are provided for students, and an excessive amount of time is spent addressing disruptions and completing managerial tasks
Mid: The teacher provides activities most of the time, but some learning time is lost in dealing with disruptions and managerial tasks
High: The teacher provides activities for the students and deals efficiently with disruptions and managerial tasks

Routines (Students know what to do, Clear instructions, Little wandering)
Low: The classroom routines are unclear; most students do not know what is expected of them
Mid: There is some evidence of classroom routines that allow everyone to know what is expected
High: The classroom resembles a 'well-oiled machine'; everybody knows what is expected and how to go about doing it

Transitions (Brief, Explicit follow-through, Learning opportunities within)
Low: Transitions are too long, too frequent, and/or inefficient
Mid: Transitions sometimes take too long or are too frequent and inefficient
High: Transitions are quick and efficient

Preparation (Materials ready and accessible, Knows lessons)
Low: The teacher does not have activities prepared and ready for the students
Mid: The teacher is mostly prepared but takes some time away from instruction for last-minute preparations
High: The teacher is fully prepared for activities and lessons

▶ View descriptors
Instructional Learning Formats: Focuses on the ways in which the teacher maximizes students' interest, engagement, and ability to learn from lessons and activities

Effective Facilitation (Teacher involvement, Effective questioning, Expanding children's involvement)
Low: The teacher does not actively facilitate activities and lessons to encourage students' interest and expanded involvement
Mid: At times, the teacher actively facilitates but at other times merely provides activities for the students
High: The teacher actively facilitates students' engagement in activities and lessons to encourage participation and expanded involvement

Variety of Modalities and Materials (Range of auditory, visual, and movement opportunities, Interesting and creative materials, Hands-on opportunities)
Low: The teacher does not use a variety of modalities or materials to gain students' interest and participation
Mid: The teacher is inconsistent in use of a variety of modalities and materials
High: The teacher uses a variety of modalities including auditory, visual, and movement and uses a variety of materials to effectively interest students

Student Interest (Active participation, Listening, Focused attention)
Low: The students do not appear interested and/or involved in the lesson or activities
Mid: Students may be engaged for periods of time, but at other times their interest wanes
High: Students are consistently interested and involved in activities and lessons

Clarity of Learning Objectives (Advanced organizers, Summaries, Reorientation statements)
Low: The teacher makes no attempt or is unsuccessful at orienting students toward learning objectives
Mid: The teacher orients students somewhat, or learning objectives may be clear during some periods but less so during others
High: The teacher effectively focuses students' attention toward learning objectives and/or the purpose of the lesson

Concept Development: Measures the teacher's use of instructional discussions and activities to promote students' higher-order thinking skills and cognition and the teacher's focus on understanding rather than on rote instruction

Analysis and Reasoning (Why and/or how questions, Problem solving, Prediction/experimentation, Classification/comparison, Evaluation)
Low: The teacher rarely uses discussions and activities that encourage analysis and reasoning
Mid: The teacher occasionally uses discussions and activities that encourage analysis and reasoning
High: The teacher often uses discussions and activities that encourage analysis and reasoning

Creating (Brainstorming, Planning, Producing)
Low: The teacher rarely provides opportunities for students to be creative and/or generate their own ideas and products
Mid: The teacher sometimes provides opportunities for students to be creative and/or generate their own ideas and products
High: The teacher often provides opportunities for students to be creative and/or generate their own ideas and products

Integration (Connects concepts, Integrates with previous knowledge)
Low: Concepts and activities are presented independent of one another, and students are not asked to apply previous learning
Mid: The teacher sometimes links concepts and activities to one another and to previous learning
High: The teacher consistently links concepts and activities to one another and to previous learning

Connections to the Real World (Real-world applications, Related to students' lives)
Low: The teacher does not relate concepts to the students' actual lives
Mid: The teacher makes some attempts to relate concepts to the students' actual lives
High: The teacher consistently relates concepts to the students' actual lives

▶ View descriptors
Quality of Feedback: Assesses the degree to which the teacher provides feedback that expands learning and understanding and encourages continued participation

Scaffolding (Hints, Assistance)
Low: The teacher rarely provides scaffolding but rather dismisses responses as incorrect or ignores problems in understanding
Mid: The teacher occasionally provides scaffolding but at other times simply dismisses responses as incorrect
High: The teacher often scaffolds for students who are having a hard time understanding a concept, answering a question, or completing an activity

Feedback Loops (Back-and-forth exchanges, Persistence by teacher, Follow-up questions)
Low: The teacher gives only perfunctory feedback to students
Mid: There are occasional feedback loops between the teacher and students; other times feedback is more perfunctory
High: There are frequent feedback loops — back-and-forth exchanges — between the teacher and students

Prompting Thought Processes (Asks students to explain thinking, Queries responses and actions)
Low: The teacher rarely queries students or prompts them to explain their thinking and rationale
Mid: The teacher occasionally queries students or prompts them to explain their thinking
High: The teacher often queries students or prompts them to explain their thinking and rationale

Providing Information (Expansion, Clarification, Specific feedback)
Low: The teacher rarely provides additional information to expand on students' understanding or actions
Mid: The teacher occasionally provides additional information to expand on students' understanding
High: The teacher often provides additional information to expand on students' understanding or actions

Encouragement and Affirmation (Recognition, Reinforcement, Student persistence)
Low: The teacher rarely offers encouragement of students' efforts that increases involvement and persistence
Mid: The teacher occasionally offers encouragement that increases involvement and persistence
High: The teacher often offers encouragement of students' efforts that increases involvement and persistence

Language Modeling: Captures the quality and amount of the teacher's use of language-stimulation and language-facilitation techniques

Frequent Conversation (Back-and-forth exchanges, Contingent responding, Peer conversations)
Low: There are few if any conversations in the classroom
Mid: There are limited conversations in the classroom
High: There are frequent conversations in the classroom

Open-Ended Questions (Questions require more than a one-word response, Students respond)

Mid: The teacher asks a mix of closed-ended and open-ended questions
High: The teacher asks many open-ended questions

Repetition and Extension (Repeats, Extends/elaborates)
Low: The teacher rarely, if ever, repeats or extends the students' responses
Mid: The teacher sometimes repeats or extends the students' responses
High: The teacher often repeats or extends the students' responses

Self- and Parallel Talk (Maps own actions with language, Maps student action with language)
Low: The teacher rarely maps his or her own actions and the students' actions through language and description
Mid: The teacher occasionally maps his or her own actions and the students' actions through language
High: The teacher consistently maps his or her own actions and the students' actions through language and description

Advanced Language (Variety of words, Connected to familiar words and/or ideas)
Low: The teacher does not use advanced language with students
Mid: The teacher sometimes uses advanced language with students
High: The teacher often uses advanced language with students
