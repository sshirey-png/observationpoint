▶ View descriptors
Positive Climate: Reflects the emotional connection between the teacher and students and among students and the warmth, respect, and enjoyment communicated by verbal and nonverbal interactions

Relationships (Physical proximity, Shared activities, Peer assistance, Matched affect, Social conversation)
Low: There are few, if any, indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another
Mid: There are some indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another
High: There are many indications that the teacher and students enjoy warm, supportive relationships with one another

Positive Affect (Smiling, Laughter, Enthusiasm)
Low: There are no or few displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students
Mid: There are sometimes displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students
High: There are frequent displays of positive affect by the teacher and/or students

Positive Communication (Verbal affection, Physical affection, Positive expectations)
Low: There are rarely positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students
Mid: There are sometimes positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students
High: There are frequently positive communications, verbal or physical, among teachers and students

Respect (Eye contact, Warm, calm voice, Respectful language, Cooperation and/or sharing)
Low: The teacher and students rarely, if ever, demonstrate respect for one another
Mid: The teacher and students sometimes demonstrate respect for one another
High: The teacher and students consistently demonstrate respect for one another

▶ View descriptors
Negative Climate: Reflects the overall level of expressed negativity in the classroom; the frequency, quality, and intensity of teacher and peer negativity are key to this scale

Negative Climate is scaled in the opposite direction. Higher negativity indicates lower quality.

Negative Affect (Irritability, Anger, Harsh voice, Peer aggression, Disconnected or escalating negativity)
Low: The teacher and students do not display strong negative affect
Mid: Mild displays of irritability, anger, or negative affect
High: Consistent irritability, anger, or negative affect

Punitive Control (Yelling, Threats, Physical control, Harsh punishment)
Low: The teacher does not yell or make threats
Mid: Occasional threats or yelling to establish control
High: Repeated yelling or threats

Sarcasm/Disrespect (Sarcastic voice/statement, Teasing, Humiliation)
Low: Not sarcastic or disrespectful
Mid: Occasionally sarcastic or disrespectful
High: Repeatedly sarcastic or disrespectful

Severe Negativity (Victimization, Bullying, Physical punishment)
Low: No instances of severe negativity
Mid: No instances of severe negativity
High: Instances of severe negativity between teacher and students or among the students

▶ View descriptors
Teacher Sensitivity: Encompasses the teacher's awareness of and responsivity to students' academic and emotional needs

Awareness (Anticipates problems and plans appropriately, Notices lack of understanding and/or difficulties)
Low: Consistently fails to be aware of students who need extra support
Mid: Sometimes aware
High: Consistently aware

Responsiveness (Acknowledges emotions, Provides comfort, Individualized support)
Low: Unresponsive or dismissive; same support to all
Mid: Sometimes responsive
High: Consistently matches support to needs and abilities

Addresses Problems (Helps effectively and timely, Helps resolve problems)
Low: Ineffective
Mid: Sometimes effective
High: Consistently effective

Student Comfort (Seeks support and guidance, Freely participates, Takes risks)
Low: Students rarely seek support
Mid: Students sometimes seek support
High: Students freely seek support and share ideas

Regard for Student Perspectives: Captures the degree to which interactions place an emphasis on students' interests, motivations, and points of view

Flexibility and Student Focus — Low: rigid, teacher-driven / Mid: sometimes follows lead / High: flexible, organizes around interests

Support for Autonomy and Leadership — Low: no support / Mid: sometimes / High: consistent support

Student Expression — Low: few opportunities / Mid: some periods / High: many opportunities

Restriction of Movement — Low: highly controlling / Mid: somewhat controlling / High: freedom of movement

Behavior Management: Encompasses the teacher's ability to provide clear behavioral expectations and use effective methods to prevent and redirect misbehavior

Clear Behavior Expectations — Low: absent/unclear / Mid: stated but inconsistently enforced / High: clear and consistently enforced

Proactive — Low: reactive / Mid: mix / High: consistently proactive

Redirection of Misbehavior — Low: ineffective / Mid: some success / High: effective, subtle cues

Student Behavior — Low: frequent misbehavior / Mid: periodic / High: few if any instances

Productivity: Considers how well the teacher manages instructional time and routines

Maximizing Learning Time — Low: few activities, excessive time lost / Mid: provides activities but some time lost / High: deals efficiently with disruptions

Routines — Low: unclear / Mid: some evidence / High: well-oiled machine

Transitions — Low: too long/frequent / Mid: sometimes long / High: quick and efficient

Preparation — Low: not prepared / Mid: mostly prepared / High: fully prepared

Instructional Learning Formats: Focuses on the ways in which the teacher maximizes students' interest, engagement, and ability to learn

Effective Facilitation — Low: does not actively facilitate / Mid: inconsistent / High: actively facilitates engagement

Variety of Modalities and Materials — Low: limited variety / Mid: inconsistent / High: uses auditory, visual, movement

Student Interest — Low: not interested / Mid: engaged for periods / High: consistently interested

Clarity of Learning Objectives — Low: unsuccessful orienting / Mid: somewhat / High: effectively focuses attention

Concept Development: Measures use of instructional discussions and activities to promote higher-order thinking

Analysis and Reasoning — Low: rarely uses / Mid: occasionally / High: often

Quality of Feedback: Assesses the degree to which the teacher provides feedback that expands learning

Scaffolding — Low: dismisses responses / Mid: occasionally / High: often scaffolds

Frequent Conversation — Low: few conversations / Mid: limited / High: frequent
