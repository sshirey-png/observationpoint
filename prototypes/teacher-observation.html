1 — Needs Improvement: Very few or no students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Very few or no students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations and/or directions through their actions. Students do not execute transitions, routines and procedures in an orderly manner. Students are left without work to do for a significant portion of the class period.

2 — Emerging: Some students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Some students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations and/or directions. Students execute transitions, routines and procedures in an orderly and efficient manner only some of the time and/or require substantial direction from the teacher. Students are idle while waiting for the teacher or left with nothing to do for one or two minutes at a time.

3 — Developing: Most students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. Most students demonstrate a clear understanding of behavioral expectations and directions. Students execute transitions, routines and procedures in an orderly and efficient manner most of the time, though they may require some direction from the teacher. Students are idle for short periods of time (less than one minute at a time) while waiting for the teacher to provide directions, when finishing assigned work early, or during transitions.

4 — Proficient: All or almost all students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses and/or ask appropriate questions. All or almost all students follow behavioral expectations and/or directions. Students execute transitions, routines and procedures in an orderly and efficient manner with minimal direction or narration from the teacher. Class has a quick pace and students are engaged in the work of the lesson from start to finish. Students who finish assigned work early engage in meaningful learning without interrupting other students' learning.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and at least one of the following types of evidence is demonstrated: Students assume responsibility for routines and procedures and execute them in an orderly, efficient and self-directed manner, requiring no direction or narration from the teacher. Students demonstrate a sense of ownership of behavioral expectations by holding each other accountable for meeting them.

1 — Needs Improvement: Few students use kind words & actions and show empathy towards their peers when working with others. Few students respond to consequences calmly. Classroom norms are minimally evident. Few students frequently participate in the use of Kagan Strategies. The teacher rarely incorporates Kagan activities. General Characteristic: Negative teacher-student or student-student interactions.

2 — Emerging: Some students use kind words & actions and show empathy towards their peers. Some students respond to consequences calmly. Classroom norms are partially evident. Some students participate in Kagan Strategies. The teacher sometimes incorporates Kagan activities. General Characteristic: Neutral teacher-student or student-student interactions.

3 — Developing: Most students use kind words & actions and show empathy towards their peers. Most students respond to consequences calmly. Classroom norms are mostly evident through student interaction. Most students participate in Kagan Strategies. The teacher almost always incorporates 2 Kagan class-building activities & 1 team-building activity every week. General Characteristic: Positive, teacher-driven teacher-student and student-student interactions.

4 — Proficient: All students use kind words & actions and show empathy. All students respond to consequences calmly. All students frequently share positive communication, affirmations, and celebrations. Classroom norms are evident through student interaction and through encouraging and celebrating risk-taking, collaboration, respect for divergent thinking and students' culture. The teacher always incorporates 2 Kagan class-building activities & 1 team-building activity every week. At least 1 Kagan structure is used in all lessons. Teacher always ends the use of a structure with a "gambit." General Characteristic: Positive, student-driven teacher-student and student-student interactions.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and at least one of the following: Students exhibit strong problem solving skills related to peers and the classroom community that enhance interactions. Students participate in Kagan structures at an exemplary level.

4 — Proficient: The lesson focuses on grade-level content. All activities are aligned to the learning goal(s) and are well-sequenced and build on each other to move students toward mastery. All lessons drive towards students answering a meaning driven question.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and the following evidence is demonstrated: Students make connections between what they are learning and other content across disciplines. Students independently connect lesson content to real-world situations.
T4
Cognitive Engagement — Are all students responsible for doing the thinking?
1NI
2Emrg
3Dev
4Prof
5Exm
▶ View rubric descriptors
1 — Needs Improvement: Very few or no students provide meaningful oral or written evidence to support their thinking. Students complete very little of the cognitive work during the lesson; the teacher completes all or almost all of the cognitive work. Students respond negatively to their peers' thinking, ideas, or answers. No students or very few try hard to complete challenging academic work. Students are engaged in discussion or written work for less than 35% of the lesson.

2 — Emerging: Some students provide meaningful oral or written evidence to support their thinking. Students complete some of the cognitive work, but the teacher or a very small number of students complete most of it. Students do not respond to their peers' thinking or provide feedback. Some students try hard to complete challenging work. Students are engaged in discussion or written work for 35-40% of the lesson.

3 — Developing: Most students provide meaningful oral or written evidence to support their thinking. Students complete an appropriately challenging amount of cognitive work given the focus of the lesson. Students respond to their peers' thinking, ideas or answers, and provide feedback. Most students try hard even if the work is challenging. Students are engaged in discussion or written work for at least 50% of the lesson.

4 — Proficient: All or almost all students provide meaningful oral or written evidence to support their thinking. Students complete an appropriately challenging amount of cognitive work. Students respond to and build on their peers' thinking, ideas or answers. Students routinely provide constructive feedback and respond productively when a peer answers incorrectly. All or almost all students consistently try hard even if the work is challenging. Students are engaged in discussion or written work for more than 50% of the lesson.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and at least one of the following: Students are able to make inferences, draw conclusions, approach/solve problems in multiple ways. Students independently show enthusiasm and interest in taking on advanced or more challenging content.

Demonstration of Learning — Do all students demonstrate that they are learning?
1NI
2Emrg
3Dev
4Prof
5Exm
▶ View rubric descriptors
1 — Needs Improvement: Questions, tasks or assessments do not yield data that allow the teacher to assess students' progress toward learning goals. Students have very few or no opportunities to express learning through academic writing and/or explanations using academic language. Very few or no students demonstrate how well they understand lesson content. Student responses, work and interactions demonstrate that most students are not on track to achieve learning goals.

2 — Emerging: Questions, tasks or assessments yield data that only partially allow the teacher to assess progress. Students have few opportunities to express learning through academic writing. Some students demonstrate understanding through work and/or responses. Student responses demonstrate that some students are on track.

3 — Developing: Questions, tasks or assessments yield data that allow the teacher to assess progress. Students have some opportunities to express learning through academic writing. Most students demonstrate understanding through work and/or responses. Most students are on track to achieve learning goals.

4 — Proficient: Questions, tasks or assessments yield data that allow the teacher to assess progress and help pinpoint where understanding breaks down. Students have extensive opportunities to express learning through academic writing and/or explanations using academic language. All students demonstrate understanding through work and/or responses. All or almost all students are on track to achieve learning goals.

5 — Exemplary: All descriptors for Level 4 are met, and at least one of the following: Students self-assess whether they have achieved the lesson objective and provide feedback to the teacher. Students monitor their own progress, identify their own errors and seek additional opportunities for practice.
